## Overview

Spaces are top-level workspaces that keep your content organized and isolated. Each space has its own types, entries, templates, and tags — nothing bleeds between spaces. Think of them like separate notebooks or projects.

When you create an account, a default space is created automatically. Guest users also get a default space. You can create as many additional spaces as you need.

## Creating a Space

1. Click the **space switcher** in the sidebar header (shows your current space name)
2. Select **Create Space**
3. Enter a name and pick an emoji icon
4. Optionally, choose an existing space to **copy types from** — this duplicates all types (and optionally their templates) into the new space as independent copies
5. Click **Create**

{/* screenshot: Create Space dialog with name, emoji, and "copy types from" option */}

Your new space is ready. The app switches to it automatically.

## Switching Spaces

Click the space switcher in the sidebar header to see all your spaces. Click any space to switch to it. Your last-used space is remembered across sessions.

Spaces shared with you by other users appear in a **Shared with you** section in the switcher.

{/* screenshot: Space switcher dropdown showing owned spaces and "Shared with you" section */}

## Content Isolation

Each space is fully independent:

- **Types** created in one space don't appear in another
- **Entries** belong to exactly one space
- **Templates** are scoped to the space they were created in
- **Tags** are unique per space — the same tag name can exist in different spaces without conflict
- **Pins** are per-user and per-space

When you switch spaces, the sidebar, dashboard, search, and all views update to show only that space's content.

## Managing Spaces

Go to **Settings > Spaces** to manage your spaces:

- **Rename** a space or change its emoji
- **Archive** a space to hide it from the switcher without deleting it (you can't archive your last remaining space)
- **Delete** a space permanently — this removes all its entries, types, templates, and tags. Deletion cannot be undone. You can't delete your last remaining space.

If you're a recipient of a shared space (not the owner), you can **leave** it from the space switcher dropdown instead of deleting it.

{/* screenshot: Settings > Spaces page showing a list of spaces with archive and delete actions */}

## Spaces and Collaboration

Sharing in Swashbuckler is per-space. When you share a space, the recipient gets access to all content in that space (subject to any [exclusions](/docs/sharing) you configure). Each collaborator can apply their own [theme](/docs/themes) to a shared space — visual preferences are personal, not shared.

See [Sharing](/docs/sharing) and [Real-Time Collaboration](/docs/realtime-collaboration) for details.
